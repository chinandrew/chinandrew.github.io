I am a Biostatistics PhD student at the Johns Hopkins Bloomberg School of Public Health, working with Aki Nishimura on Markov Chain Monte Carlo methods for high dimensional data.

Previously, I was a statistical developer with the Delphi Group in the Carnegie Mellon Machine Learning Department, building pipelines and tools for COVID-19 surveillance and forecasting. Before that, I worked in industry as a product manager and software engineer at Datavant.

I received my Bachelor's and Master's degrees in statistics from UC Davis, where I studied large scale network data under James Sharpnack.
